The Single Gate That Builds Supercomputers 😲

You might think a supercomputer is packed with thousands of different, complex parts.

But if you stripped it down to its bare bones, you’d only find one repeating pattern.

Now, computers process logic using AND, OR, and NOT gates.

But manufacturing billions of different microscopic gates isn't efficient.

So, engineers use Universal Gates instead.

You see, a NAND gate only shuts down when both of its inputs are active.

And by wiring multiple NAND gates together into precise geometric shapes—

You can build any other gate you want.

Meaning everything you see on a screen

—Are built entirely on a single, simple act of rejection.
